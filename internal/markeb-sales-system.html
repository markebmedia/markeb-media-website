The 5-stage sales system

Every deal follows the same path. Your job is to move prospects through each stage as fast as possible — meetings are where revenue is made, not messages.

1
Lead Generation
Objective — Start a conversation with a decision-maker
Lead sources
  • Cold calls
  • LinkedIn / Instagram outreach
  • Email outreach
  • In-person walk-ins
  • Referrals
Target contacts
  • Valuers
  • Branch Managers
  • Agency Owners
2
Proposal Message
Objective — Book a meeting
Key principles
  • Position Markeb as a marketing solution
  • Introduce the free shoot offer
  • Create curiosity — do not oversell
  • Never send booking links immediately
What you're selling at this stage

Not a service. Not a package. Just 10 minutes of their time. Everything else comes in the meeting.

Example message

"We help estate agents attract more listings and generate stronger buyer interest through high-quality marketing. We're currently offering a free shoot when agents join our system — worth showing you how it works properly. Are you free today or tomorrow for a quick 10 minutes?"

3
The Meeting
Objective — Build trust and guide the client into our system
Meeting types
  • In-person (always preferred)
  • Online via Teams
Rules
  • Book same-day or next-day where possible
  • Always speak to a decision-maker
  • Be confident and structured
This is the most important stage

The meeting is where the relationship is built and the decision is made. Everything before it is just getting through the door. Everything after it is follow-through.

4
Product Brief
Objective — Position Markeb as the default solution
During the meeting you must
  • Understand their business
  • Identify their marketing gaps
  • Recommend the correct services
Important

Do not let the client choose randomly. Lead the decision confidently. Use the service cards in this system to guide the conversation.

Positioning line

"For properties like yours, this is what we recommend to get the best result."

5
Close — First Booking
Objective — Get the client into the ecosystem
Every meeting must end with
  • A confirmed booking, or
  • A scheduled follow-up with a clear next step
Follow-up message

"We still have your free shoot available — do you have a property coming up we can get booked in?"

Key rule

Do not leave a meeting without a next step. Momentum is everything — a warm lead that goes cold is a wasted meeting.

Pipeline rules

No lead sits without action

Every lead must be either booked for a meeting or actively followed up. Nothing stays static.

14-day rule

If no meeting has been secured within 14 days of first contact:

  • Push hard for a meeting
  • Or remove from the pipeline entirely

Follow-up sequence

Every lead requires:

  • Immediate response on first contact
  • 24-hour follow-up if no reply
  • 3–5 total touchpoints before removal

Handling objections

Most objections aren't objections — they're requests for more information or reassurance. Understand what's behind them and respond honestly. Never try to overcome them — try to understand them.

Price
It's more than we usually spend on photography.
That's a fair point to raise. It's worth understanding what you're currently getting for what you spend — and whether it's delivering what you need. Our pricing reflects professional-grade equipment, specialist travel, and post-production editing that makes a genuine difference to how a property presents. If budget is a constraint, it's worth starting with what would make the biggest difference to your listings and working from there. What's the one thing you feel your current photography is missing?
Commitment
We're not sure we need this for every listing.
That's completely reasonable — and honestly, not every property needs the same level of content. It's worth thinking about which properties in your portfolio would benefit most from professional photography or video, and starting there. We can work with you on a per-property basis rather than a blanket approach if that makes more sense for how you operate.
Relevance
Our vendors don't really ask for video.
That's interesting — do you think that's because they don't see it as an option, or because they genuinely don't value it? In our experience, vendors rarely ask for video specifically, but they respond very positively when they see it. It also tends to benefit the agent as much as the vendor — it's content you can use for your own marketing, not just the listing.
Timing
We'd like to think about it and come back to you.
Of course — it's not a decision to rush. It'd help to know what would make it easier to come to a decision. Is there something specific you'd want to think through, or something I haven't covered that would help? I'm happy to send something over in writing so you've got it to refer back to.
Competition
We've had cheaper quotes from other photographers.
It's worth comparing like for like — what's included, what the editing looks like, and what the turnaround is. We're not the cheapest option, and we wouldn't want to be. If price is the main factor, we might not be the right fit. But if the quality of output and reliability of the service matters — which for most agents it does — it's worth looking at what the difference actually buys you.
Personal branding
I'm not sure I'm comfortable on camera.
That's one of the most common things we hear — and it's exactly why the session is directed. You're not left to figure it out yourself. Our specialists guide you through the whole thing, and most people find they're far more comfortable than they expected once they're in it. Would it help to talk through what the session actually looks like in practice?

Principles

The mindset behind every successful interaction. Come back to these when things aren't going to plan.

Meetings over messages

A message can be ignored. A meeting cannot. Every action you take should be designed to get you in front of a decision-maker, not to close them through a screen.

Speed over perfection

A meeting booked today is worth more than a perfectly crafted message sent next week. Move fast, follow up faster.

Confidence over complexity

You don't need a perfect answer for everything. You need the confidence to hold the room, recommend with conviction, and guide the client to a decision.

Consistency over intensity

150 outreaches every day beats 500 in one day and nothing the next. The system works when you work it consistently.

You are not selling photos

You are selling a marketing system that helps agents win more listings, attract more buyers, and operate more consistently. Lead every conversation from that position.
