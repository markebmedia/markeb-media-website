The Case Study Method — Why This Works
Case studies are the single most powerful tool in our sales process — a warm intro referencing an existing client converts at a fraction of the effort of cold outreach. The marketing system takes that exact same principle and applies it to content. Every piece of content we publish is proof. Real clients. Real reactions. Real results. It makes every viewer feel like they already know us before we've ever spoken to them.
Primary creative inspiration — content style reference
@jonnydodge on Instagram
Jonny Dodge built an audience and an inbound pipeline by posting phone call content — he's on the phone with clients trying to book his services, the call plays on loudspeaker, the camera captures his reaction in real time. Style 1 — The Voicenote / Phone Conversation
Inspired by @jonnydodge · Raw, real, high-converting · Executed by SMM

The format: A client sends us a voicenote — a booking request, a reaction to their content delivery, feedback after a result. We take that voicenote and turn it into phone conversation content. The phone is on loudspeaker. The camera acts as a third wall — recording the person listening to the message in real time. Their reaction is the content.

Step 1 — Head of Operations asks the client after a shoot or delivery
HoO has the most regular face-to-face contact with clients — after a shoot, after content is delivered, after an amendment is resolved. That's the moment to make the ask. The conversation is warm, it's personal, and the timing is right because the client has just experienced our service.
High-converting voicenote types to ask for
  • Reaction to their delivered content
  • A result — listing sold, offer received, buyer enquiry spike
  • Booking again — wanting another shoot
  • Recommending us to another agent
  • Feedback on the overall experience
The incentive — 20% off their next shoot
  • HoO generates a unique discount code for that client
  • Code is valid for 30 days from the date of the ask
  • Applies to any service booking — no minimum
  • Only offered in exchange for the voicenote
  • One code per client — keeps it exclusive
"[Name], really glad you're happy with how it came out. Would you mind sending me a quick voicenote — just 20–30 seconds saying what you thought of the content? We're building out our social and it makes a massive difference coming from someone real. As a thank you I'll send you a code for 20% off your next booking — valid for 30 days."
Always get verbal confirmation they're happy to be featured before posting. Offer to tag them — most clients want the exposure.
Step 2 — Film it: phone on loudspeaker, camera as the third wall
The person on camera is listening to the voicenote play back on loudspeaker. The camera is set up in front of them. Their face, their reaction — that's what the viewer sees. The voicenote is the audio. The reaction is the content.
Shot setup
  • Phone on loudspeaker — visible in frame
  • Presenter looking at the phone, not the camera
  • Camera is the silent third wall — no breaking it
  • Natural lighting or minimal ring light — keep it real
  • Clean background or branded office space
Who's on camera
  • Social Media Manager — primary presenter
  • Head of Marketing — for variety
  • BDM — when the message relates to a sales win
  • CEO — for major milestones or big testimonials
  • Rotate faces — keep it feeling like a team, not one person's channel
Don't edit the voicenote. The rawness is the proof — polished audio sounds like an ad. Unedited sounds like a real person.
Step 3 — Edit and post: punchy, raw, subtitled
SMM edits and posts. HoM approves the caption angle. The edit should feel natural, not over-produced.
Edit style
  • Subtitles on — always, no exceptions
  • Voicenote audio clear and loud
  • Minimal music — ambient only or none
  • 30–60 seconds max
  • Hook in the first 2 seconds
Caption structure
  • Hook line — what the message is about
  • One line of context — who / what service
  • CTA — "DM us to book" or "Link in bio"
  • Relevant estate agent / property hashtags
The viewer hears a real client, sees a real reaction. It's not an ad. It's proof. This is the format that turns viewers into inbound enquiries.

Style 2 — In-Person Client Conversations
Light-hearted or podcast-style · At the office or on location · Executed by SMM

The framing: We invite the client to record a podcast episode — for their personal brand. We produce it, we film it, we edit it and give it to them. That's the value they get. We happen to weave in conversation topics that give us the case study content we need. It never feels like an interview about us — it's their episode, their content, their brand. We just benefit from what comes out naturally.

How the conversation works: The episode topics are about the client — their journey, their market, their approach to property. Our questions naturally draw out moments where Markeb comes up: "How has your listing presentation changed?" "What do you do differently now?" The client talks about their results. We get the case study. They get a polished podcast episode for their LinkedIn, Instagram, and YouTube. Everyone wins.

Step 1 — Head of Marketing plans the episode topics
HoM builds the episode around the client's story — their journey, wins, approach to the market. The topics are framed as their personal brand content. The case study moments are drawn out through the right questions, not scripted in.
Episode topic angles
  • "How I approach marketing a listing in [region]"
  • "The shift that changed how I present to vendors"
  • "What separates top agents from average ones"
  • "My process from valuation to completion"
  • "What I wish I'd done differently early in my career"
Questions that draw out case study moments

  • "What do you do now that you didn't do 12 months ago?"
  • "Can you think of a listing where the marketing made the difference?"

The episode is framed entirely around them. Markeb comes up naturally — it's never the subject, it's part of the answer.
Step 2 — SMM films the podcast session
Two camera angles where possible — one on each person. The SMM leads the conversation using HoM's episode brief. Feels like a proper podcast recording, not a corporate sit-down.
Setup
  • Two cameras — one on each speaker
  • Lapel mics or a single condenser between them
  • Landscape — YouTube and LinkedIn native
  • 30–45 minutes of conversation
  • Clean background or branded office environment
What the client gets
  • Full episode edit for their YouTube or LinkedIn
  • 3–5 short clips cut for their Reels and TikTok
  • We produce it and hand it over — they own it
  • Their name and brand front and centre
One 30-minute session = a full episode for the client + up to 7 short-form clips for Markeb. One filming day fills a full week of content.
Step 3 — SMM edits both outputs, HoM approves angles, post daily
SMM produces the full episode cut for the client and cuts Markeb's short-form clips separately. HoM writes a different caption angle for every clip. Each one is a different perspective from the same conversation — not the same content reposted.
Their personal brand episode. Our case study content. The client gets a polished podcast episode for free. We get a week of proof content. One session, two wins.

Broadcast — Scheduled sends to active clients
Monday & Thursday Availability Broadcast
Fires twice a week to all active clients with email notifications enabled. Each client receives real available slots from their own region — not a generic message. This is what keeps Markeb front of mind between shoots.

Trigger: Netlify scheduled function (scheduled-weekly-broadcast.js) runs daily at 14:00 UTC — fires email only on Monday (day 1) and Thursday (day 4) · Condition: Client's Email Notifications Enabled = true in Airtable · Sent via: Resend · From: commercial@markebmedia.com

Step 1 — Pull active clients from Airtable
Function queries Airtable for all records where Email Notifications Enabled = true. Any client without that flag is excluded and will never receive the broadcast.
Step 2 — Fetch real availability per region
For each client the function hits the check-availability endpoint using their postcode and region. It checks the next 7 weekdays and filters for days with available slots. Up to 5 days of availability are included — each showing available times for that date.
If no availability exists in a client's region the email tells them to check live availability in the dashboard. If the client has no region set they're prompted to update their profile.
Step 3 — Personalised send per client
Subject: Available shoot dates in [Region] — book your slot. Opens with their first name. Lists the available days and times for their region. Single CTA to book via the dashboard.
"Hi [Name], we have availability in [Region] coming up: Monday 26th: 9am, 11am, 2pm · Wednesday 28th: 10am, 3pm · Friday 30th: 9am. Ready to book your next shoot? Book now →"
Line breaks convert to br tags so spacing renders correctly across all email clients. The 7-day window deliberately avoids "this week" phrasing since it can span two calendar weeks.

Admin control: The Email Notifications toggle in the admin panel maps directly to the Email Notifications Enabled field in Airtable. Toggling it off immediately removes that client from all future broadcasts — no code changes needed.
