Leaving the Office

Never leave the office without telling someone or clarifying where you're going, whether that's five minutes or the rest of the day.

How to tell someone

A quick message in Teams or the team WhatsApp is enough. It doesn't need to be a conversation. Say what you're doing and roughly when you'll be back. A verbal "heads up" to whoever's in the room doesn't count on its own if no one actually hears it, so put it in writing too.

e.g. "Popping to the bank, back in 20" / "Heading straight to a shoot from here, back around 3"
What this covers
  • Short breaks, lunch, errands, and early finishes, not just full days out
  • Heading straight to or from a shoot without coming back to the office
  • Working from home or anywhere other than the office on a given day
Locking up

If you're the last one out, make sure the office door is properly shut and secured behind you, and windows are closed before you leave. If you're not sure whether you're last, check before you go.

Core Working Hours

Pick one of the three 8 hour windows below and stick to that same window every working day, so the team always knows when to expect you. If your circumstances change and you need to switch windows, agree it with management first rather than changing day to day.

8:00 to 4:00
Early start
8:30 to 4:30
Mid start
9:00 to 5:00
Standard start
Lunch break

These windows already include your lunch break. An 8:00 to 4:00 day is 8 hours in total, lunch included, not 8 hours on top of a break. Take it when it makes sense around shoots and meetings, but you're still expected back at your desk (or on site) within the window.

Running late

If you're going to be late starting your window for any reason, message the team as early as you can, ideally before the time you'd normally start. Persistent lateness without warning is a conversation with management, not something that just slides.

Shoots and travel

Shoots, client meetings, and travel outside these hours are a normal part of the role and aren't a breach of this policy. This section is about the baseline in office window on days without early or late commitments.

🤒

Sickness & Absence

If you're not going to make it in, whether that's sickness, an emergency, or otherwise, let management know directly, as early as possible and before your usual start time. A message to the general team chat isn't enough on its own for a full day absence; someone with visibility of the day's bookings needs to know so shoots can be covered or moved.

  • Sickness: message directly before your start time where possible
  • Planned time off: agree it in advance, not on the day
  • If you're booked on a shoot that day, flag this clearly so it can be reassigned or rescheduled
🎬

Content Turnaround

All content from a shoot must be sent to the editors on the same calendar day as the shoot, not "before you personally finish for the day," and not pushed to the next morning.

Deadline

Content should be sent as soon as reasonably possible after the shoot wraps, and no later than the end of your working day. If a shoot finishes very close to end of day, get it uploaded that evening rather than leaving it overnight.

How it's sent

Use WeTransfer or Dropbox to upload content, as set up per client, not personal devices, personal cloud storage, or messaging apps. If you're unsure which method applies to a job, check before the shoot, not after.

If something goes wrong

If you can't upload on the day, whether that's no signal on site or a technical fault, message management the same day to flag it, rather than staying quiet and sending it late. Flagged delays are understood; unflagged ones aren't.

🧹

Keeping the Office Tidy

The office should be kept clean and tidy throughout the day, not just tidied up at the end of it.

  • Clear your own desk and any shared spaces (kitchen, meeting area) straight after you use them
  • Wash up and put away any dishes, mugs, or kitchen equipment you use, don't leave it for someone else
  • Empty a bin as soon as it's full to the rim, rather than waiting for it to overflow, don't leave it for "someone else" to notice first
👥 Whose job is it? Everyone's. Anyone in the office can and should do this, regardless of role or seniority
📷

Equipment & Kit Care

Cameras, drones, laptops, and other kit are shared company property and need to be looked after so the next person isn't let down.

  • Batteries are charged and put back on charge after use, not left flat for the next shoot
  • Memory cards are cleared and content backed up before being reused
  • Kit is returned to its proper storage place after every shoot, not left in cars or at home overnight
  • Any damage, faults, or near misses (including with the drone) are reported to management immediately, not after the next job reveals it
🔐

Security & Access

Access cards and door codes are not to be shared outside the team or lent to anyone without checking with management first. Report a lost or missing access card the same day it happens.

The office should never be left open and unattended, even briefly. If you're stepping out and someone else isn't clearly staying, make sure the door is properly shut behind you.

🔒

Client Data & Confidentiality

Client details, property access information, and unreleased content are confidential. This includes property addresses, key codes, and anything in Airtable or a client's dashboard.

  • Don't discuss client jobs, prices, or personal details outside the team or on personal social media
  • Don't share raw or unedited content with anyone outside the company before it's been through the proper delivery process
  • Lock your laptop screen when you step away from your desk
🚗

Driving & Site Safety

You're responsible for driving safely and legally between shoots, including having valid insurance, licence, and MOT for any vehicle used for work.

  • No phone use while driving, including for satnav changes; set your route before you set off
  • Drone flights follow CAA rules at all times, no exceptions for a "better shot"
  • If a property or site feels unsafe to enter or shoot (access issues, aggressive dogs, hazards, anything that doesn't feel right), leave and call the office rather than pushing through it
🤝

Client Visitors

If a client or visitor is coming into the office, whoever arranged the visit is responsible for making sure the space is presentable beforehand. This sits on top of, not instead of, the general tidiness expectations above. Give the team a heads up in good time so everyone can help if needed.

⚠️

If This Isn't Followed

Most of this comes down to communication and consideration for the rest of the team. A quiet reminder is usually all that's needed the first time something slips.

Repeated or ignored breaches

Where something keeps happening after it's been raised, whether that's persistent lateness, repeated late content delivery, ignoring the tidiness expectations, or leaving without telling anyone, it becomes a direct conversation with management, and may be treated as a performance or conduct issue.
